<objective>
Teach the Go watcher to recognise CCC ONE *closed* repair orders (final bill paid + RO completed) and emit `DocumentStatus="C"` in the BMS XML so the server's existing `CLOSED_STATUSES` branch fires the **review** job instead of the (wrong) 24h+3day estimate sequence.

Today every CCC ONE export carries `ENV.TRANS_TYPE = "E"` regardless of whether the RO is fresh or finalised — Marco's diagnostic showed 40/40 POSTs in the last 2 weeks were "E". The actual close indicators live in two sibling dBase files the parser currently ignores:

- `*.ad2` — `RO_CMPDATE` (RO completion date) and `DATE_OUT` (vehicle departure)
- `*.ttl` — `G_TTL_AMT` (grand total / final bill amount)

A bundle is "closed" iff (`RO_CMPDATE` non-empty OR `DATE_OUT` non-empty) AND `G_TTL_AMT > 0`. The first half says the RO was marked done; the second half ensures we don't confuse "estimate-only saved with a stale completion date" with an actual final invoice.

Server-side `app.py` (lines 104-105, 2782-2799) already routes `DocumentStatus ∈ {I,C,F,FI,FC,WC}` to the review-job branch — **no server change needed**, just have the watcher tell the truth.

Purpose: stop sending "thanks for the estimate, here's a 3-day follow-up" texts to customers whose vehicles are already paid for and out the door. Send the review request instead.

Output:
- `internal/ems/bundle.go` + `parse.go` + `bms.go` updated with AD2/TTL parsing and the closed-RO override
- table-driven tests covering the 4 canonical cases + a 5th (DATE_OUT-only with bill)
- a rebuilt + signed `EarlScheibWatcher-Setup.exe` at the repo root, committed in the same release-style commit pattern as `df55201`, ready for Marco's self-update loop to pick up within ~7 minutes
